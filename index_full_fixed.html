Humane Gopher Control

Start here if you want to relocate or deter gophers without killing. Effectiveness can vary by soil, season, and food sources.

Live Trapping & Relocation

  • Identify fresh mounds (dark, moist soil) and probe to locate main tunnels 6–12 inches deep.
  • Place two‑door live traps in the main tunnel, wired to a stake. Mark with a flag for visibility.

Castor‑Oil Soil Treatment

Castor‑oil based repellents make soil/tubers unappealing without harming animals. Apply with a hose‑end sprayer, then water in 6–8 inches. Re‑apply after heavy rain.

Physical Barriers & Raised Beds

Install hardware cloth (½‑inch galvanized) under raised beds or as vertical fences 18–24 inches deep. Overlap seams by at least 6 inches and secure with landscape staples.

Ultrasonic & Vibrational Stakes

Mixed results. Use as a supplement with trapping or soil treatment. Place every 30–45 feet; move weekly to avoid habituation.

Non‑Humane (Lethal) Methods

Legal & Safety Disclaimer: Lethal control may be restricted where you live. Use only approved products, follow label directions, and consider humane options first. Keep traps and baits away from pets, children, and non‑target wildlife.

Kill Traps (Box/Pincher Type)

  • Probe for the main runway near fresh mounds; excavate a 4–6 inch opening and square the tunnel.
  • Set two traps back‑to‑back facing opposite directions in the main tunnel; anchor with wire.
  • Cover the opening to block light; check every 12–24 hours. Reset until activity stops.

Zinc Phosphide or Anticoagulant Baits

For licensed/allowed use only. Place in main tunnels only using a probe and bait applicator. Never surface‑broadcast. Remove dead animals if found to reduce secondary risk.

Gas Cartridges & Aluminum Phosphide (Fumigation)

Restricted use in many areas. Follow label, seal all openings, and observe re‑entry intervals. Consider professional services for this method.

Ineffective or Risky Methods (Use Caution)

  • Flooding: Often fails; can damage soil structure.
  • Exhaust/Smokers: Legal restrictions; variable effectiveness; safety hazards.
  • Glass shards/poisons not labeled for gophers: Never use.

Frequently Asked Questions

What’s the fastest way to get rid of gophers?

Correctly placed box/pincher kill traps on the main runway usually work in 24–72 hours. Combine with barrier repairs to prevent reinfestation.

Is relocation legal where I live?

Relocation is regulated in many states. Call local wildlife control or check your state’s wildlife agency website before transporting animals.

Do ultrasonic stakes work?

Sometimes as a supplement. They’re best paired with trapping or castor‑oil treatment.

How do I know tunnels are active?

Fresh mounds are dark and crumbly; plug a hole with soil — if it’s reopened in 24 hours, it’s active.
