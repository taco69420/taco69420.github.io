## Quick Answer
Gophers avoid plants with strong-smelling roots, toxic compounds, or fibrous root systems they can't easily chew. Swapping high-risk plants (roses, fruit trees, root vegetables) for resistant varieties in gopher-heavy zones won't eliminate the problem but it will dramatically reduce the damage and the replanting cycle.

## Gopher-Resistant Plants Worth Knowing

**Good choices for California yards:**
- Lavender — fragrant roots gophers avoid
- Rosemary — same deal, and it handles the heat
- Salvia (most native varieties) — low palatability
- Catmint (Nepeta) — repels gophers and looks good doing it
- Euphorbia — toxic sap makes it a hard pass for gophers
- Agapanthus — not their favorite, decent as a border plant
- Breath of Heaven (Coleonema) — fragrant, avoided consistently
- Gopher plant (Euphorbia lathyris) — specifically named for this, though it can spread aggressively

**High-risk plants to protect or relocate away from active zones:**
- Roses
- [Fruit trees](/problems/tree-shrub-damage) (especially young ones)
- [Root vegetables](/problems/vegetable-garden) — carrots, beets, sweet potatoes
- Tulip and other soft bulbs
- Young ornamental trees

## The Process
1. Walk your yard and identify which plants are taking repeated gopher damage.
2. For high-value plants you can't replace (mature fruit trees, established roses), shift to <a href="https://amzn.to/4b9UG87" target="_blank" rel="sponsored noopener">hardware cloth barriers</a> rather than hoping gophers will leave them alone.
3. When replanting damaged areas, choose from the resistant list above where possible.
4. Use resistant plants as a border around garden beds — a ring of lavender or rosemary won't stop a gopher but it reduces entry points.
5. Combine plant selection with [active trapping](/methods/victor-traps) in any zone where you've had consistent pressure.

## Bagger Pro-Tip
"Gopher-resistant" doesn't mean gopher-proof. A hungry gopher in a bad year will eat things it normally ignores. Use resistant plants as a risk-reduction layer, not a replacement for trapping.

## Safety Warning
Euphorbia sap is a skin and eye irritant. Wear gloves when handling and keep it away from areas where kids or pets dig.
