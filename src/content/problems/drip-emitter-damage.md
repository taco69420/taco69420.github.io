## Quick Answer
Gophers don't target drip emitters specifically — they chew them because they're soft plastic obstacles in the way of tunneling. But the result is the same: dead zones in your irrigation, soggy spots where water is dumping underground, and a repair bill that keeps coming back until the gopher is gone. The emitter fix takes ten minutes. The gopher fix takes a [trap](/traps).

## The Gear
- Replacement drip emitters (match GPH rating of originals)
- Barbed fittings and goof plugs
- Utility knife
- Needle-nose pliers
- Small wire mesh scraps for protection

## The Process
1. Run your irrigation and walk the zone. Dead plants with wet soil nearby or a pressure drop at the valve are the two signs of a chewed emitter or line.
2. Locate the damaged emitter — look for a hole in the tubing, a chewed emitter body, or a fitting that's been pulled loose.
3. Pull the old emitter and inspect the barb fitting underneath. If the barb is intact, just press a new emitter in. If the tubing is chewed at the fitting, cut back to clean tubing and install a new barbed elbow.
4. Before reconnecting, press a small piece of [hardware cloth](/methods/gopher-mesh) loosely around the fitting to discourage repeat gnawing at the same spot.
5. Eliminate the gopher. If you only fix the emitter, you'll be fixing it again in 2–3 weeks.

## Bagger Pro-Tip
Buy a bag of 50 replacement emitters and keep them in the garage. If you're in active gopher territory, you will use them. Buying one at a time from the hardware store is a tax on not being prepared.

## Safety Warning
If your drip system runs near low-voltage landscape lighting wire, hand-dig carefully when tracing damaged lines. [Gophers chew wire](/problems/irrigation-damage) too — and a nicked lighting cable buried in wet soil is a problem you don't want.

## Fix It
Fix the emitter, then fix the gopher problem.
- [Victor Trap Tunnel Intercept](/methods/victor-traps) — set traps in the run nearest the damaged emitter
- [Cinch Trap Mainline Set](/methods/cinch-traps) — good alternative for tight spaces near irrigation lines
- [Install Gopher Mesh Barriers](/methods/gopher-mesh) — wrap repaired sections in hardware cloth for lasting protection
